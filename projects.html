Linear Transformations as Geometry: Projection, Eigen-Directions, and the SVD

A geometric exposition of linear transformations. Projection leads to the least-squares solution; eigenvectors describe invariant directions; positive definite structure appears through quadratic forms; and the singular value decomposition synthesizes the picture as rotation–scaling–rotation. The notebook emphasizes structure and interpretation, with minimal but purposeful computation.
